## About Wattr

Wattr is a software intelligence platform for data centres. It integrates IT-layer data — servers, GPUs, workload schedules — with facility-layer telemetry from chillers, cooling towers, and pumps to optimise energy and water consumption. The core idea is to shift from reactive control to predictive, proactive optimisation: anticipating thermal and load conditions rather than responding to them after the fact. The approach draws on predictive cooling work at hyperscale operators and extends it to include water efficiency and ESG attribution at the asset level.

## My long-term direction

The long-term goal is to build AI systems that are physically grounded, computationally efficient, and deployable at infrastructure scale. That means moving beyond benchmark-driven ML towards systems that account for energy cost, hardware constraints, and real-world feedback loops. Neuromorphic and hybrid architectures are one piece of this; reinforcement learning for closed-loop control in physical environments is another. The common thread is building intelligence that operates under the constraints of the real world rather than around them.
